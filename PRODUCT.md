# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Photographers and privacy-conscious users who want a local-first photo library. They are sitting at their desk or on the couch, managing personal photo collections — importing, browsing, organizing by people and places, searching, and occasionally editing. They care about keeping their photos private and off the cloud, but they also want a fast, beautiful experience that doesn't feel like a compromise.

## Product Purpose

Prism replaces cloud photo services with a local-first desktop app that indexes, organizes, searches, and secures photo libraries without sending data anywhere. Success is a photo library that feels personal, fast, and trustworthy — where users know their memories stay on their machine.

## Brand Personality

Warm, personal, intimate. Prism should feel like opening a private photo album — not like logging into a tool. Quiet confidence, not corporate polish. The interface should recede and let photos feel like the most important thing on screen.

## Anti-references

Generic SaaS dashboards — cream backgrounds, identical card grids, gradient text, numbered section eyebrows, warm-neutral monoculture. Prism should never feel like a landing page or a productivity app. Also avoid: Google Photos (corporate, algorithmic feed), Lightroom (intimidating, pro-only complexity).

## Design Principles

1. **Privacy is the product.** Local-first and encrypted by default. The security model should feel visible and reassuring, not hidden or technical.
2. **Photos are the hero.** UI chrome recedes. The photo grid, lightbox, and editing views should dominate attention. Every design decision should answer: does this help the photo speak?
3. **Warmth through craft.** Intimate, personal feel — serif typography, considered spacing, subtle texture. Not cold, not corporate. The kind of detail that makes someone feel like this was made for them.
4. **Power without complexity.** Rich features (albums, people, maps, AI search, locked folder, editing) wrapped in an approachable shell. Complexity is available, never forced.
5. **Trust through transparency.** Users should feel confident their data stays local. Security and privacy should be felt in the design, not just documented.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA target for core browsing and navigation flows
- Reduced motion support via `prefers-reduced-motion` for all animations
- Keyboard navigation for gallery grid, lightbox, and sidebar
- Screen reader support for photo metadata and album management
- High contrast mode consideration for OLED displays
